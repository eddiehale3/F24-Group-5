Overview

Princeton University is a private Ivy League research university located in Princeton, New Jersey. Founded in 1746, it is one of the oldest universities in the United States and is renowned for its academic excellence, historic campus, and commitment to research and public service.

Location

Princeton University is located at Nassau St, Princeton, NJ 08544, USA. The campus covers about 600 acres and features a mix of Collegiate Gothic architecture and modern facilities.

Academic Programs

Princeton offers a range of undergraduate and graduate programs across various departments and schools, including:

  • School of Engineering and Applied Science
  • Woodrow Wilson School of Public and International Affairs
  • Department of Humanities and Social Sciences
  • Princeton School of Architecture
  • Princeton Neuroscience Institute
  • Princeton Environmental Institute
  • Department of Natural Sciences

Admission Requirements

Admission to Princeton is highly selective. Requirements include:

  • Common Application or Coalition Application
  • High school transcript
  • School Report and counselor recommendation
  • Two teacher recommendations
  • Standardized test scores (optional)
  • Personal essays
  • Extracurricular activities and honors

Tuition

For the 2021-2022 academic year:

  • Undergraduate tuition: $56,010
  • Room and board: $17,780
  • Other expenses (books, supplies, etc.): $3,500

Note: Princeton meets 100% of demonstrated financial need for all admitted students.

Facilities

Princeton University offers a variety of academic, cultural, and athletic facilities, including:

  • Firestone Library
  • Princeton University Art Museum
  • Princeton Stadium
  • Richardson Auditorium
  • Lewis Center for the Arts
  • Princeton Plasma Physics Laboratory
  • University Chapel
