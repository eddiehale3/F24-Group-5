Overview

Columbia University is a private Ivy League research university located in New York City. Founded in 1754, it is one of the oldest universities in the United States and is known for its academic rigor, prominent faculty, and historical significance.

Location

Columbia University is located in the Morningside Heights neighborhood of Manhattan, at 116th St & Broadway, New York, NY 10027, USA. The urban campus spans 36 acres and offers a vibrant city experience with access to numerous cultural and professional resources.

Academic Programs

Columbia offers a diverse range of undergraduate and graduate programs through its various schools and colleges, including:

  • Columbia College
  • Fu Foundation School of Engineering and Applied Science
  • Columbia Law School
  • Vagelos College of Physicians and Surgeons
  • Columbia Business School
  • Graduate School of Journalism
  • Graduate School of Architecture, Planning and Preservation
  • School of International and Public Affairs

Admission Requirements

Admission to Columbia University is highly selective. Requirements include:

  • Common Application or Coalition Application
  • High school transcript
  • Letters of recommendation
  • SAT or ACT scores (optional for 2021-2022)
  • Essays, including Columbia-specific prompts
  • Extracurricular activities

Tuition

For the 2021-2022 academic year:

  • Undergraduate tuition: $63,530
  • Room and board: $15,450
  • Other expenses (books, supplies, etc.): $2,000

Note: Columbia meets 100% of demonstrated financial need for admitted students.

Facilities

Columbia University offers world-class facilities, including:

  • Butler Library
  • Low Memorial Library
  • Avery Architectural and Fine Arts Library
  • Dodge Fitness Center
  • Columbia University Medical Center
  • Lenfest Center for the Arts
