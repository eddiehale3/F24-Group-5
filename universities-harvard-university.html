Overview

Harvard University is a private Ivy League research university located in Cambridge, Massachusetts. Founded in 1636, it is the oldest institution of higher education in the United States. Harvard is renowned for its academic prestige, extensive resources, influential alumni, and consistently ranks among the worldâ€™s top universities.

Location

Harvard University is located at 1350 Massachusetts Avenue, Cambridge, MA 02138. The university's campus covers approximately 5,000 acres, including the main campus in Cambridge, the Harvard Medical School in Boston, and other properties throughout the Boston area.

Harvard University offers a diverse array of undergraduate and graduate programs across twelve schools:

  • Harvard Business School
  • Harvard Divinity School

  • Harvard John A. Paulson School of Engineering and Applied Sciences

  • Harvard Medical School
  • Harvard School of Dental Medicine
  • Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health
  • Radcliffe Institute for Advanced Study

Admission Requirements

Admission to Harvard is highly competitive. Requirements include:

  • Common Application, Coalition Application, or Universal College Application
  • High school transcript
  • Two letters of recommendation from teachers
  • School Report and mid-year school report
  • SAT or ACT scores (optional)
  • Personal essays
  • Extracurricular activities and achievements

Tuition

For the 2021-2022 academic year:

  • Undergraduate tuition: $54,768
  • Room and board: $18,389
  • Other expenses (books, supplies, etc.): $3,500

Note: Harvard meets 100% of demonstrated financial need for admitted students.

  • Harvard Art Museums
  • Harvard Stadium
  • Sanders Theatre

  • Harvard Medical School and its affiliated teaching hospitals
