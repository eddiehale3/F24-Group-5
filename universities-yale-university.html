Overview

Yale University is a private Ivy League research university located in New Haven, Connecticut. Founded in 1701, it is the third-oldest institution of higher education in the United States and is renowned for its rigorous academics, historic campus, and commitment to public service.

Location

Yale University is located at 105 Wall Street, New Haven, CT 06511, USA. The campus spans over 1,000 acres in downtown New Haven, blending historic Gothic architecture with modern facilities.

Academic Programs

Yale offers a wide range of undergraduate and graduate programs across several schools and colleges:

  • Yale College (Undergraduate)
  • Graduate School of Arts and Sciences
  • School of Medicine
  • School of Law
  • School of Management
  • School of Art
  • School of Music
  • School of Architecture
  • School of Public Health
  • Divinity School
  • School of Forestry & Environmental Studies
  • School of Drama
  • Jackson School of Global Affairs

Admission Requirements

Admission to Yale is highly competitive. Requirements include:

  • Common Application, Coalition Application, or QuestBridge Application
  • High school transcript
  • Two teacher recommendations and a counselor recommendation
  • Standardized test scores (optional)
  • Personal essays
  • Extracurricular activities and achievements

Tuition

For the 2021-2022 academic year:

  • Undergraduate tuition: $59,950
  • Room and board: $17,200
  • Other expenses (books, supplies, etc.): $3,700

Note: Yale meets 100% of demonstrated financial need for all admitted students.

Facilities

Yale University boasts historic and modern facilities, including:

  • Sterling Memorial Library
  • Beinecke Rare Book & Manuscript Library
  • Yale University Art Gallery
  • Yale Center for British Art
  • Payne Whitney Gymnasium
  • Yale Repertory Theatre
  • Yale-New Haven Hospital
