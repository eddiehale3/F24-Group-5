Overview

The California Institute of Technology (Caltech) is a private research university in Pasadena, California. Founded in 1891, Caltech is renowned for its strength in engineering and the physical sciences and its commitment to cutting-edge research and innovation.

Location

Caltech is located at 1200 E California Blvd, Pasadena, CA 91125, USA. Its campus spans approximately 124 acres, featuring a blend of historic and modern facilities within a compact, focused environment.

Academic Programs

Caltech offers a variety of undergraduate and graduate programs, primarily focused on science and engineering disciplines, including:

  • Division of Biology and Biological Engineering
  • Division of Chemistry and Chemical Engineering
  • Division of Engineering and Applied Science
  • Division of Geological and Planetary Sciences
  • Division of Humanities and Social Sciences
  • Division of Physics, Mathematics and Astronomy
  • Jet Propulsion Laboratory (managed by Caltech for NASA)

Admission Requirements

Admission to Caltech is highly selective. Requirements include:

  • Common Application or Coalition Application
  • High school transcript
  • Letters of recommendation from teachers
  • Standardized test scores (optional for 2021-2022)
  • Personal essays
  • Extracurricular activities, with an emphasis on STEM involvement

Tuition

For the 2021-2022 academic year:

  • Undergraduate tuition: $58,680
  • Room and board: $17,691
  • Other expenses (books, supplies, etc.): $2,235

Note: Caltech offers need-based financial aid and meets 100% of demonstrated financial need for admitted students.

Facilities

Caltech features state-of-the-art facilities, including:

  • Millikan Library
  • Beckman Institute
  • LIGO (Laser Interferometer Gravitational-Wave Observatory)
  • Caltech Seismological Laboratory
  • Cahill Center for Astronomy and Astrophysics
  • Broad Center for the Biological Sciences
