Overview

The University of Pennsylvania (Penn) is a private Ivy League research university located in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. Founded in 1740 by Benjamin Franklin, Penn is one of the oldest universities in the United States and is known for its interdisciplinary approach and prestigious professional schools.

Admission Requirements

Admission to the University of Pennsylvania is highly competitive. Requirements include:

  • Common Application or Coalition Application with Penn-specific supplement
  • High school transcript
  • Letters of recommendation
  • SAT or ACT scores (optional for 2021-2022)
  • Essays and personal statement
  • Extracurricular activities and achievements

Tuition

For the 2021-2022 academic year:

  • Undergraduate tuition: $61,710
  • Room and board: $17,180
  • Other expenses (books, supplies, etc.): $1,358

Note: Penn meets 100% of demonstrated financial need for admitted students.
