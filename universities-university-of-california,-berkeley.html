Overview

The University of California, Berkeley (UC Berkeley) is a public research university located in Berkeley, California. Founded in 1868, it is the flagship campus of the University of California system and is widely recognized for its academic excellence, research contributions, and commitment to social impact.

Location

UC Berkeley is located at 200 California Hall, Berkeley, CA 94720, USA. The campus spans approximately 1,232 acres, with views overlooking the San Francisco Bay.

Academic Programs

UC Berkeley offers a wide range of undergraduate and graduate programs across fourteen colleges and schools:

  • College of Letters and Science
  • College of Engineering
  • Haas School of Business
  • School of Law
  • College of Chemistry
  • College of Environmental Design
  • School of Public Health
  • Graduate School of Education
  • School of Information
  • Goldman School of Public Policy

Admission Requirements

Admission to UC Berkeley is highly competitive. Requirements include:

  • University of California Application
  • High school transcript
  • Personal insight questions
  • Letters of recommendation (optional for most applicants)
  • SAT or ACT scores (optional)
  • Extracurricular activities and achievements

Tuition

For the 2021-2022 academic year:

  • In-state undergraduate tuition: $14,226
  • Out-of-state undergraduate tuition: $44,008
  • Room and board: $18,500
  • Other expenses (books, supplies, etc.): $1,500

Note: UC Berkeley offers need-based financial aid and meets financial need for in-state students.

  • Jacobs Institute for Design Innovation
