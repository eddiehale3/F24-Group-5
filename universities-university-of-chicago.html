Overview

The University of Chicago is a private research university located in Chicago, Illinois. Founded in 1890, it is known for its rigorous academic programs, influential research, and commitment to open inquiry and critical thought.

Location

The University of Chicago is located at 5801 S Ellis Ave, Chicago, IL 60637, USA. Its campus spans approximately 217 acres in the Hyde Park neighborhood, with gothic-style architecture and modern facilities.

Academic Programs

The University of Chicago offers a broad array of undergraduate and graduate programs across its divisions and schools, including:

  • College of the University of Chicago
  • Booth School of Business
  • Law School
  • Pritzker School of Medicine
  • Harris School of Public Policy
  • Division of the Humanities
  • Division of the Physical Sciences
  • Division of the Social Sciences
  • Divinity School

Admission Requirements

Admission to the University of Chicago is highly competitive. Requirements include:

  • Common Application or Coalition Application
  • High school transcript
  • Letters of recommendation
  • SAT or ACT scores (test-optional policy available)
  • Essays, including the unique UChicago supplement
  • Extracurricular activities

Tuition

For the 2021-2022 academic year:

  • Undergraduate tuition: $60,963
  • Room and board: $17,004
  • Other expenses (books, supplies, etc.): $2,500

Note: The University of Chicago offers need-based financial aid and meets 100% of demonstrated financial need for admitted students.

Facilities

The University of Chicago is home to numerous world-class facilities, including:

  • Joseph Regenstein Library
  • Smart Museum of Art
  • Gleacher Center
  • Mansueto Library
  • Ratner Athletics Center
  • University of Chicago Medical Center
